Q1 SRTF vs SJF Statement: "Shortest remaining time first (SRTF) behaves like shortest job first (SJF) if the set of processes is fixed (no new processes) and they are completely CPU-bound." Answer: True.. Why: SRTF is the preemptive version of SJF. If the process set is fixed (no new arrivals) and processes are CPU-bound (no I/O that causes intermediate blocking/arrivals), the preemption never happens in ways that change the SJF ordering - the schedules coincide. Q2 - CPU-bound vs I/O-bound and effect on context switches Difference (simple): CPU-bound process: does long CPU bursts, rarely issues I/O. It holds the CPU for long stretches. . I/O-bound process: does short CPU bursts and frequently does I/O (blocks often) Effect on context switching: I/O-bound processes cause more frequent context switches because they block for I/O and the OS must switch to another process often. They produce many short CPU bursts (more scheduler decisions) before switching). If preempted by time quantum, they still cause switches but less frequently than many small I/O bursts.. (So I/O-bound context-switch rate; CPU-bound context-switch frequency but each switch may cost the same overhead.) Q3 - Counting semaphore / one erroneous process.
