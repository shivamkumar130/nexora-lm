[CS203] Design and Analysis of Algorithms Course Instructor: Dr. Dibyendu Roy Autumn 2024-25 Scribed by: Shivam Kumar (202351130) Lecture (Week 5) 1 Dynamic Programming (DP) Dynamic Programming (DP) is a method used for solving complex problems by breaking them down into simpler subproblems. It is applicable when the subproblems overlap, meaning the same subproblems are solved multiple times. The main idea is to store the results of these subproblems to avoid redundant calculations. 1.1 Key Concepts A. Optimal Substructure: A problem exhibits optimal substructure if an optimal solution can be constructed from optimal solutions of its sub- problems. B. Overlapping Subproblems: A problem has overlapping subproblems if the same subproblems are solved multiple times. C. Memoization vs. T abulation: • Memoization (T op-Down): Recursively solves subproblems and caches their results. • T abulation (Bottom-Up):Iteratively solves subproblems and stores results in a table. 1.2 Key Steps in Dynamic Programming When developing a dynamic programming algorithm, we typically follow these four steps: • A. Characterize the Structure of an Optimal Solution. • B. Recursively Define the V alue of an Optimal Solution. • C. Compute the V alue of an Optimal Solution. • D.
