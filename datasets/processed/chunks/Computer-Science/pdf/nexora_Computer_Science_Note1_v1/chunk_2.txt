Resistive Respiration Sensor Interface. A two-pin connector (J_SENsOR1) is used to interface the external resistive respiration sensor. SENsOR+: Connected to the excitation and measurement path. SENsOR-: Connected to system ground Breathing causes small resistance changes in the sensor, which are converted into voltage. variations by the analog front-end. 4. Analog Front-End (AFE) 4.1 Constant Current Source (U2A) Op-Amp: TLV9062 (Unit A) Function: Generates a stable constant current through the resistive sensor .R_SET (33 kQ): Sets the excitation current value This approach ensures that sensor resistance variations are linearly converted into voltage changes. 4.2 Signal Amplifier (U2B) Op-Amp: TLV9062 (Unit B) Function: Amplifies the small voltage variations from the sensor R_G (10 kQ) & R_F (100 kQ): Configure the amplifier gain This stage improves signal amplitude while maintaining low noise performance 4.3 ADC Input Filtering Before feeding the signal to the MCU ADC: :R1 (100 Q): Series resistor :C2 (100 nF): Shunt capacitor to ground Together, they form a low-pass Rc filter that suppresses high-frequency noise and stabilizes ADC sampling. Output net: ADC IN 5. Microcontroller Unit (MCU) 5.1 ESP32-C3-WROOM-02 The ESP32-C3 serves as the central controller and provides: 12-bit ADC for analog signal acquisition Digital processing and filtering Wireless communication (Bluetooth / Wi-Fi). 5.2 Supporting Components .C1 (0.1 F): Local decoupling capacitor R3 (10 kQ): EN pin pull-up resistor The conditioned analog signal is sampled through the ADc_IN pin.. 6. Power Management Unit 6.1 Power Input J2: 5V input from USB or external supply. 6.2 Voltage Regulation U3 (AMS1117-3.3): Linear regulator providing 3.3V. C4, C5 (10 F): Bulk capacitors for stability .C6, C7 (0.1 F): High-frequency decoupling capacitors. This ensures a stable and low-noise supply for both analog and digital circuits. 7.
