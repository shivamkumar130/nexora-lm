During the process, the array is split into four regions, which may or may not contain elements. These regions evolve as the function progresses. At the start of each iteration of thefor loop (lines 3–6), the regions maintain specific properties, which we express as a loop invariant. 2.3 Loop Invariant At the beginning of each iteration of the loop, for any array index k: 1. If p ≤ k ≤ i: - All elements in this range A[k] are less than or equal to the pivot x. 2. If i + 1 ≤ k ≤ j − 1: - All elements in this range A[k] are greater than the pivot x. 3. If k = r: - A[k] = x, meaning the pivot remains unchanged at position r. These conditions ensure that the array is gradually being divided into two parts—elements smaller than or equal to the pivot and elements larger than the pivot—while the pivot stays in place at the end of the array. Once the loop finishes, the pivot is swapped into its correct position between the two partitions. 3.
