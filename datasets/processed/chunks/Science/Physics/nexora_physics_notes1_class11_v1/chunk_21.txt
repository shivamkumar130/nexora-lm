Resultant Vector Definition The single vector that produces the same effect as two or more vectors acting together is called the resultant vector. Suppose A⃗ and B⃗ act simultaneously. Their resultant is: R⃗ = A⃗ + B⃗ Importance of Resultant Instead of analyzing several vectors separately, they can be replaced by one equivalent vector. This simplifies calculations. Example Force 1 = 10 N East Force 2 = 15 N East Resultant: 25 N East 2. Vector Addition Vector addition is the process of combining two or more vectors to obtain a resultant vector.
