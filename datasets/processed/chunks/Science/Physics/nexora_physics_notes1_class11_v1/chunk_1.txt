UNIT–1: UNITS AND DIMENSIONS 1. Physical Quantity Definition A physical quantity is any quantity that can be measured. Examples: Length Mass Time Temperature Force Energy Physical quantities are divided into two categories: (A) Fundamental Quantities These quantities are independent and do not depend on any other physical quantity. Examples: 1. Length 2. Mass 3. Time 4. Temperature 5. Electric Current 6. Luminous Intensity 7. Amount of Substance (B) Derived Quantities These quantities depend upon one or more fundamental quantities. Examples: Area Area = Length × Breadth Dimension: [L²] Speed Speed = Distance / Time Dimension: [LT⁻¹] Volume Volume = Length × Breadth × Height Dimension: [L³] 2. Unit Definition A unit is the physical standard by which a physical quantity is measured. Example: Length → metre Mass → kilogram Time → second Units are classified as: (A) Fundamental Units Units corresponding to fundamental quantities. Examples: metre (m) kilogram (kg) second (s) (B) Derived Units Units corresponding to derived quantities. Examples: Newton Joule Pascal Watt 3. Systems of Units Different systems of units have been used throughout history. 1. CGS System Base Units: Centimetre (cm) Gram (g) Second (s) 2. MKS System Base Units: Metre (m) Kilogram (kg) Second (s) 3. FPS System Base Units: Foot (ft) Pound (lb) Second (s) 4. SI System SI (International System of Units) is the modern and universally accepted system.
