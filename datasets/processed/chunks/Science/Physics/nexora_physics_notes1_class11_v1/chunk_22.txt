Triangle Law of Vector Addition Statement If two vectors are represented in magnitude and direction by two sides of a triangle taken in the same order, then the third side taken in the opposite order represents their resultant. Understanding the Statement Suppose: A⃗ and B⃗ are two vectors. Place the tail of B⃗ at the head of A⃗. The vector joining the starting point of A⃗ to the ending point of B⃗ gives the resultant. Geometrical Construction Step 1: Draw vector A⃗ Step 2: From the head of A⃗ draw vector B⃗ Step 3: Join the initial point of A⃗ to the final point of B⃗. This line represents resultant: R⃗ Mathematical Representation R⃗ = A⃗ + B⃗ Why Triangle Law Works The two vectors form two sides of a triangle. The third side naturally represents their combined effect. Hence resultant is obtained geometrically. 4.
