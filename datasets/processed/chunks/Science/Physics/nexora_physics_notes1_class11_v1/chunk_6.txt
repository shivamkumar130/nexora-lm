COMMONLY USED PHYSICAL QUANTITIES Force Definition: Force is an external agency that changes or attempts to change the state of motion of a body. Formula: F = ma SI Unit: Newton (N) Dimension: [MLT⁻²] Work Definition: Work is said to be done when force causes displacement. Formula: W = Fs SI Unit: Joule (J) Dimension: [ML²T⁻²] Energy Definition: Capacity to perform work. SI Unit: Joule Dimension: [ML²T⁻²] Power Definition: Rate of doing work. Formula: P = W/t SI Unit: Watt Dimension: [ML²T⁻³] Pressure Definition: Force acting per unit area. Formula: P = F/A SI Unit: Pascal Dimension: [ML⁻¹T⁻²] Momentum Definition: Product of mass and velocity. Formula: p = mv SI Unit: kg m/s Dimension: [MLT⁻¹] MEMORY CHART FOR DIMENSIONS Start with Velocity Velocity = Distance/Time = [LT⁻¹] Acceleration = Velocity/Time = [LT⁻²] Force = Mass × Acceleration = [MLT⁻²] Work = Force × Distance = [ML²T⁻²] Power = Work/Time = [ML²T⁻³] This chain helps remember dimensions quickly.
