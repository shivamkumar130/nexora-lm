Parallelogram Law of Vector Addition Statement If two vectors acting simultaneously on a particle are represented in magnitude and direction by two adjacent sides of a parallelogram drawn from a common point, then the diagonal passing through that common point represents the resultant vector. Explanation Suppose two vectors A⃗ and B⃗ originate from the same point O. A parallelogram is constructed using these vectors as adjacent sides. The diagonal passing through O gives the resultant vector. Construction Step 1 Draw vector A⃗. Step 2 Draw vector B⃗ from the same origin. Step 3 Complete the parallelogram. Step 4 Draw the diagonal through O. This diagonal represents the resultant. Mathematical Representation R⃗ = A⃗ + B⃗ Importance Parallelogram law provides a graphical method of adding vectors acting at a common point.
