Mean Absolute Error The arithmetic mean of all absolute errors is called mean absolute error. Formula: Δa = (|Δa₁| + |Δa₂| + |Δa₃| +... + |Δaₙ|)/n Example Absolute errors: 0, 1, 1, 0 Mean absolute error: Δa = (0 + 1 + 1 + 0)/4 Δa = 2/4 Δa = 0.5 cm Therefore, Measurement = (10 ± 0.5) cm 12. Relative or Fractional Error The ratio of mean absolute error to mean value is called fractional error. Formula: Fractional Error = Δa/aₘ Properties: No unit Dimensionless quantity Example Mean value = 10 cm Mean absolute error = 0.5 cm Fractional error: = 0.5/10 = 0.05 13. Percentage Error Percentage error is obtained by multiplying fractional error by 100. Formula: Percentage Error = (Δa/aₘ) × 100 Example Percentage Error = (0.5/10) × 100 = 5% Therefore: Percentage Error = 5% 14. Significance of Error Analysis Error analysis helps: Determine reliability of measurements Estimate uncertainty Compare experimental results Improve accuracy Important Definitions Measurement: Comparison of unknown quantity with a standard quantity. Error: Difference between measured value and true value. Absolute Error: Difference between mean value and measured value. Mean Absolute Error: Average of absolute errors. Fractional Error: Ratio of absolute error to mean value. Percentage Error: Fractional error multiplied by 100.
