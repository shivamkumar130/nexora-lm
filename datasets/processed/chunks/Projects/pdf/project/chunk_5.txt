CONCLUSION This study successfully developed and evaluated multiple LSTM-based architectures for forecasting commodity prices across 11 essential commodities in India. Using 1,460 daily records, we demonstrated that the Attention LSTM model achieved superior performance with a validation loss of 0.018162, MAE of 0.027, RMSE of 0.039, andR 2 of 0.91, outperforming standard LSTM, Bi-LSTM, and Conv-LSTM variants. The model was trained for up to 200 epochs using the Adam optimizer with adaptive learning rate schedul- ing, effectively capturing complex temporal dependencies and inter-commodity correlations. Key contributions include: (1) a comprehensive comparison of LSTM variants on multivari- ate commodity price data, (2) robust training methodology with Batch Normalization and gradient clipping to prevent overfitting, and (3) empirical validation demonstrating that attention mechanisms significantly improve prediction accu- racy for multidimensional time series. The framework provides MSMEs with actionable early-warning capabilities to antici- pate price fluctuations during volatile market conditions such as pandemics, geopolitical conflicts, and climate events. Future work will explore Transformer-based architectures for longer forecasting horizons and incorporate exogenous variables such as weather patterns, policy changes, and global market indi- cators to further enhance predictive accuracy.
