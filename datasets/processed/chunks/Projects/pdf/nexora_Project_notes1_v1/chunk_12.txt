System Architecture The MI transmitter includes a high-Q coil, a resonant driver, and sometimes a magnetic core to boost performance.e The receiver uses a multi-turn coil, a resonance circuit, and a low-noise amplifier to detect very weak magnetic signals. Together, these components allow reliable communication in harsh littoral conditions. 4. Modulation MI systems usually use ASK, FSK, PSK, or MSK modulation. At frequencies below 6 kHz, we typically achieve 10 to 500 bps, which is suitable for underwater sensing, short messages, and navigation. 5. Historical Experiments Several important field tests established the reliability of Ml communication. In 1997, signals were successfully transmitted over more than 1300 yards in shallow water. In 1998, seabed tests showed ranges beyond 300 meters, and in 2001 UBV-sized systems achieved 16-300 bps over 250-400 meters. 6. Major Advances (2005-2025) Recent research introduced compact coil designs, metamaterial resonators, relay-aided networks, and MI waveguides made of passive resonant loops. MI is also used for underwater localization, achieving 0.5 to 1 meter accuracy, even where acoustic systems fail. 7. Challenges Despite its advantages, MI has limitations such as 1/R3 signal decay, narrow bandwidth, and sensitivity to coil orientation. Environmental conductivity also affects performance. 8. Conclusion In summary, MI communication offers a stable, predictable, and energy-efficient solution for shallow-water and mixed-media environments.
