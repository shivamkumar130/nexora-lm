Introduction With the rapid advancement of Industry 4.0, smart cities, and cyber-physical systems, Digital Twins have become a foundational technology for representing, monitoring, and optimizing real-world systems in virtual environments. A digital twin establishes a continuous, bidirectional connection between a physical system and its virtual counterpart, enabling real-time monitoring, simulation, and decision support. However, most existing digital twin platforms rely on 2D dashboards or static 3D CAD. models, which are insufficient for understanding complex spatial structures, dense. environments, and dynamic system behavior. These limitations reduce situational awareness At the same time, Knowledge Graphs (KGs) provide a structured semantic representation of entities, attributes, and relationships, enabling intelligent querying, reasoning, and inference. diagrams, disconnected from real-world geometry.. Meanwhile, modern sensing technologies such as LiDAR and photogrammetry generate dense 3D point cloud data, offering highly accurate geometric representations of real environments. However, interacting with massive point clouds using traditional interfaces is computationally expensive and cognitively challenging. This project proposes an Immersive Digital Twin Platform that integrates Knowledge Graph reasoning with real-time 3D point cloud visualization inside VR/AR environments, enabling intuitive, semantic, and spatial interaction with complex digital twins.. 2. Geometry-Semantics Disconnection Geometric data (3D models, point clouds) and semantic data (knowledge graphs, rules) 2. Non-Immersive Interaction Traditional 2D dashboards and flat 3D views fail to convey spatial complexity, reducing human understanding and increasing cognitive load. 3. These issues significantly limit the practical use of digital twins in smart manufacturing, infrastructure monitoring, smart cities, and cyber-physical systems. 3.
