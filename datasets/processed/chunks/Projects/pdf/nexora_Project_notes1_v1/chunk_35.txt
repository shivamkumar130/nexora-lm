Modulation Schemes in MI Classical modulation schemes applicable: ASK, FSK, PSK. Examples: o Tone modulation -> simple device control o FsK -> binary data transmission (demonstrated at 10% of carrier frequency) o Minimum Shift Keying (MsK) -> more bandwidth-efficient FSK, higher data rates o PSK -> high data rates possible (~25% of carrier frequency) Here's a simplified explanation of Modulation Schemes in MI systems Applicable Modulation Schemes ASK (Amplitude Shift Keying): Varies the amplitude of the magnetic field to encode data. FSK (Frequency Shift Keying): Changes the frequency of the magnetic field for binary data. PSK (Phase Shift Keying): Changes the phase of the magnetic field to encode data. MSK (Minimum Shift Keying): A more efficient version of FSK for higher data rates. Examples of Use. Tone modulation: Simple on/off signals for controlling devices FsK: Binary data transmission; demonstrated at 10% of the carrier frequency.. PSK: Can achieve very high data rates, up to ~25% of the carrier frequency.. Summary MI systems can use classical digital modulation schemes to transmit data. The choice of scheme balances data rate, bandwidth, and complexity for the specific application. 10.
