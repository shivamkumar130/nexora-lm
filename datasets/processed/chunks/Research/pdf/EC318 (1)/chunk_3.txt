Therefore, breathing should be continuously and accurately monitored, as it is very important for our daily healthy life. Different sensing methods are used to monitor respiration patterns, like temperature sensors and strain-based sensors [13], [14]. Some traditional methods like polysomnography are still used in hospitals to detect respiration patterns [11]. To detect this, sensors and tubes are attached to the body, which is quite uncomfortable [15]. Chest belts are also used in a way to detect respiration monitoring. these also affect body movement and create a lot of noise [15]. Other methods like camera-based systems and ultrasonic sensors measure breathing patterns indirectly [16], [17] with other sensor such as fiber-optic sensors are also used for respiration monitoring [18]–[20]. Due to high sensitivity and fast response,There are few sensors like humidity sensor is used for monitoring the breathing pattern [21], [22]. When a person breathes, i.e., inhales, the chest expands, and when exhales, the chest contracts [15]. All these movements are detected to generate an estimated breathing pattern.These do not give accurate results of the breathing pattern [15], [23].There are some devices that we use, like face masks or nasal tubes. When these are used on the body to detect the breathing pattern, they feel quite uncomfortable and, along with that, they cannot be used on the body for a long time [15]. We have discussed earlier that traditional methods such as polysomnography, nasal tubes, chest belts, camera monitoring, and ultrasonic sensors were mostly used to detect respiration patterns [11], [15]. However, these are quite expensive, uncomfortable, and difficult to use for longer durations [24], [25]. In many cases, due to body movements [15], [26] and system limitations, inaccurate breathing patterns are obtained.
