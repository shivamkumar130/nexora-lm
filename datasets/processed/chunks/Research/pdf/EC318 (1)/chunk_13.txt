Breathing Signal Characterization The respiration signals, which were collected from the sen- sor were first analyzed to understand their actual behavior of samples. The recorded voltage signal shows periodic variations corresponding to breathing cycles. Figure 7 shows a sample breathing waveform obtained from the sensor over time period. The fluctuations in the signal represent the breathing changes occurring during respiration. Different breathing patterns shows us significant differences in their signal characteristics. Shallow breathing typically produces smaller amplitude variations, whereas Deep breath- ing shows larger amplitudes over a period. While Normal breathing lies between these two conditions. B. Data Characterization For machine learning based classification, several statistical features were extracted from each signal segment units. These features important properties of the respiration waveform and helps us distinguish between different breathing patterns. Fig. 7. Breathing signal waveform captured from the respiration sensor (V oltage vs Time). The extracted features were mean, standard dev, maxi- mum, minimum, amplitude (difference between maximum and minimum values), mean gradient, and signal variance. These statistical descriptions summarize the distribution and variations of respiration signal over time. The collected dataset has 3 breathing categories: shallow, normal, and deep breathing. Approximately 12000 samples were collected for shallow breathing and deep breathing each, while around 6000 samples were used for normal breathing. This distribution follows a 1:2:2 ratio as recommended during the data collection process. C. Machine Learning Model Performance A Random Forest classifier was used to classify breathing patterns based on there extracted features over segments or window size of 5 seconds. Random Forest was selected due to its ability to capture nonlinear relationships within the dataset.
