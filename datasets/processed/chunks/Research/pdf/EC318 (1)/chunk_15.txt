Feature Space Visualization To better understand how the extracted features differentiate breathing patterns, the distribution of samples was visualized in the feature space. Figure 11 Shows the relationship between amplitude and variance for the three breathing classes. Fig. 11. Feature space distribution showing separation of shallow, normal, and deep breathing samples. The figure reveals that different breathing patterns occupy different regions in the feature space. Deep breathing samples generally shows higher amplitude and variance values due to stronger breathing, whereas shallow shows lower amplitude regions. Normal breathing samples fall between these two clusters. This separation in the feature space explains why the Random Forest classifier can effectively distinguish between the three respiration patterns. G.
