Respondents Filling Survey Without Reading Some participants might complete surveys quickly without thoroughly understanding the questions, potentially leading to inaccurate or unreliable data. If older generations are underrepresented, the study might not fully capture generational differences in technology adoption and usage, affecting the generalizability of the. results. 3.6 Statistical Analysis. We applied various statistical analyses relevant to the relationships between some of the factors determining technological development and changes in societal values. For those factors, such as the use of digital media, implementation of Al, professional environments, and sustainability, we estimated the effects. Specifically, we applied a multivariable regression model combined with additional tests in order to test the 60 relationships between the variables and the change in societal expectations. The applied models and analysis for the study follow:. Model 0: Technology adoption (social media, Al, digital tools) and demographic factors (age, profession, education). Model 1: Model 0 added with the frequency and intensity of using social media, concentrating on its impact on personal relationships and community norms. Model 2: Model 0 with Al infusion in the workplace setting-health care or service fields again to measure the effects on professionalized communication norms. Model 3: Model 0, incorporating additional sustainability transitions-in this example, the links technology with environmental behavior norms.. Model 4: Model 0 with cyberbullying and the matter of privacy concern, mainly based. on Al and the ethics of digital technologies directed toward privacy norms..
