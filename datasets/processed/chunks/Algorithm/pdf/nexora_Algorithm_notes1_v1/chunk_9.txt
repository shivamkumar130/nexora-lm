Breadth-First Search (BFS) Breadth-First Search (BFS) is a fundamental graph traversal algorithm that explores the vertices of a graph layer by layer. It is widely used in various applications, such as finding the shortest path in an unweighted graph, determining connectivity, and building spanning trees.
