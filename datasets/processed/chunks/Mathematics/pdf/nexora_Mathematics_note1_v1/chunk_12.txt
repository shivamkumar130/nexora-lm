Parabola Notes - Advanced Examples (Session 3) Focus of a Parabola Example: Find the focus of 4y² − 6x − 4y = 5. Complete the square: 4(y²−y)=6x+5 4[(y−1/2)²−1/4]=6x+5 4(y−1/2)²=6(x+1) Standard form: (y−k)²=4a(x−h) Here: h = −1, k = 1/2 4a = 6 ⇒ a = 3/2 Focus: (h+a, k) = (1/2, 1/2) Directrix of a Shifted Parabola Example: y² + 4y + 4x + 2 = 0 (y+2)² = −4(x+1/2) Comparing with: (Y)² = −4aX a = 1 Vertex = (−1/2, −2) Directrix: x = 1/2 Vertex Condition Problem Find a if the vertex of y = x² + 2ax + 10 lies 1 unit above the x-axis. Rewrite: y=(x+a)²−a²+10 Vertex: (−a, 10−a²) Since vertex is 1 unit above x-axis: 10−a² = 1 a² = 9 a = ±3 Mirror Image of Directrix For parabola: y² = 4(x+1) Directrix: x = −2 Reflection in line: x + 2y = 3 Final reflected line: 3x − 4y + 16 = 0 Focus and Directrix Concept If focus is at origin and directrix is x+2=0, then vertex is midpoint between focus and directrix. Focus = (0,0) Directrix = x = −2 Vertex = (−1,0) Equation from Vertex and Focus Vertex = (1,1) Focus = (3,1) Distance a = 2 Standard form: (y−1)² = 4a(x−1) Therefore: (y−1)² = 8(x−1) Latus Rectum Example Endpoints of latus rectum: (6,7) and (6,−1) Midpoint: (6,3) Length = 8 4a = 8 ⇒ a = 2 Possible vertex: (4,3) or (8,3) depending on opening direction.
