Topic 1: Cofactors and Minors
Definition of Minor:
The minor Mij of an element aij is the determinant obtained by deleting the ith row and jth
column.
Definition of Cofactor:
Cij = (-1)^(i+j) Mij
Key Idea:
Cofactors follow the checkerboard sign pattern:
+ - +
- + -
+ - +
The notebook examples evaluate minors and cofactors directly and verify their values.
Topic 2: Adjoint Matrix
The adjoint of a matrix is the transpose of the cofactor matrix.
Steps:
1. Find all cofactors.
2. Form the cofactor matrix.
3. Take transpose.
Important relation:
A × adj(A) = |A| I
Topic 3: Determinants formed by Cofactors
The handwritten work constructs a determinant using cofactors such as:
(bc−a²), (b²−ac), (ab−c²), (a²−bc), (ac−b²).
Concept:
Use cofactor identities and symmetry properties of determinants to simplify large
expressions.
Topic 4: Cramer's Rule (2 Variables)
For:
a1x+b1y=c1
a2x+b2y=c2
Δ = |a1 b1; a2 b2|
Δx = |c1 b1; c2 b2|
Δy = |a1 c1; a2 c2|
Solutions:
x = Δx/Δ
y = Δy/Δ
Condition:
Δ ≠ 0
Example 29
Solve:
x+y=4
3x−2y=9
Using Cramer’s Rule:
Δ = -5
Δx = -17
Δy = -3
Therefore:
x = 17/5
y = 3/5
Topic 5: Cramer's Rule (3 Variables)
For:
a1x+b1y+c1z=d1
a2x+b2y+c2z=d2
a3x+b3y+c3z=d3
x = Δ1/Δ
y = Δ2/Δ
z = Δ3/Δ
where Δ1, Δ2 and Δ3 are obtained by replacing the corresponding variable column by
constants.
Example 30
Solve:
x+y+z=9
2x+5y+7z=52
2x+y−z=0
Using determinants:
Δ = -4
Δ1 = -4
Δ2 = -12
Δ3 = -20
Hence:
x = 1
y = 3
z = 5

Equation of Circle (Centre-Radius Form)
x² + y² = a²
(x-h)² + (y-k)² = a²
Centre = (h,k), Radius = a
General Equation of Circle
x² + y² + 2gx + 2fy + c = 0
Centre = (-g,-f)
Radius = √(g²+f²-c)
Diameter Form
(x-x1)(x-x2) + (y-y1)(y-y2) = 0
Parametric Form
For x²+y²=a²:
x=a cosθ, y=a sinθ
For (x-h)²+(y-k)²=a²:
x=h+a cosθ, y=k+a sinθ
Special Conditions
Touches x-axis: (x-h)²+(y-k)²=k²
Touches y-axis: (x-h)²+(y-k)²=h²
Touches both axes: (x-h)²+(y-k)²=h² (h=k)
Passes through origin: x²+y²+2gx+2fy=0
Intercept form: x²+y²-ax-by=0
Intercepts
Length of x-intercept = 2√(g²-c)
Length of y-intercept = 2√(f²-c)
Position of Point
For circle x²+y²=r²:
S=x₁²+y₁²-r²
S>0 Outside
S=0 On Circle
S<0 Inside
Position of Line
Distance p from centre:
p>r => No intersection
p=r => Tangent
p<r => Secant
Tangent Forms
Point form for x²+y²=a²:
xx₁+yy₁=a²
General circle:
T=0
Parametric tangent:
x cosθ + y sinθ = a
Slope Form of Tangent
y = mx ± a√(1+m²)
For centre (h,k):
(y-k)=m(x-h) ± a√(1+m²)
Point of Contact
(±am/√(1+m²), ±a/√(1+m²))
Normal
Normal passes through centre of the circle.
Radius is perpendicular to tangent at point of contact.
Power of a Point
PA·PB = PT²
Length of tangent from point (x,y): √S₁
Chord of Contact
Equation: T = 0
Chord with Given Midpoint
Equation: T = S₁
Pair of Tangents
SS₁ = T²
Important Results
Length of chord of contact = 2RL/√(R²+L²)
Area of quadrilateral formed by pair of tangents and radii = RL
Area of triangle formed by pair of tangents = PT₁T₂
Angle Between Tangents
tanθ = 2RL/(L²-R²)
Circumcircle of Triangle PT₁T₂
(x-x₁)(x+g) + (y-y₁)(y+f) = 0

Circle - Complete Master Notes
Director Circle
Locus of intersection point of perpendicular tangents.
For a circle, director circle is concentric with radius = √2 × original radius.
Position of Two Circles
d > r1+r2: 4 common tangents
d = r1+r2: 3 common tangents
|r1-r2| < d < r1+r2: 2 common tangents
d = |r1-r2|: 1 common tangent
d < |r1-r2|: 0 common tangents
Length of Common Tangents
Direct common tangent = √(d²-(r1-r2)²)
Transverse common tangent = √(d²-(r1+r2)²)
Orthogonal Circles
Two circles intersect orthogonally when angle between tangents is 90°.
For S1=x²+y²+2g1x+2f1y+c1=0 and S2=x²+y²+2g2x+2f2y+c2=0
Condition: 2(g1g2+f1f2)=c1+c2
Radical Axis
Locus of points having equal tangent lengths to two circles.
Equation: S−S' = 0
Properties of Radical Axis
1. For intersecting circles, common chord = radical axis.
2. For touching circles, common tangent at contact point = radical axis.
3. Radical axis is perpendicular to line joining centres.
4. Radical axis bisects common tangents.
5. Concentric circles have no radical axis.
6. If two circles cut a third circle orthogonally, their radical axis passes through centre of
third circle.
Radical Centre
Radical axes of three circles intersect at one point called radical centre.
Family of Circles
Family through intersection points of S=0 and S'=0:
S + λS' = 0
Family through intersection of circle S=0 and line L=0:
S + λL = 0
Family through two fixed points (x1,y1), (x2,y2): standard determinant form.
Family Touching a Fixed Line
(x−x1)² + (y−y1)² + λ[(y−y1) − m(x−x1)] = 0
