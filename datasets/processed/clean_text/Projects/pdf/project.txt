Multicommodity Price Prediction using LSTM
Variants
Shivam Kumar (202351130), Dev Saraswat (202351173), Tammineni Harika (202351145)
Abstract—This report presents a deep learning approach for
predicting commodity prices in India during the COVID-19
pandemic using Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks.
The study addresses the challenges faced by Micro, Small, and
Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) due to fluctuating commodity
prices. A multivariate LSTM model with the Adam optimizer
and Batch Normalization demonstrates superior performance,
achieving low Mean Absolute Error (MAE) while reducing
overfitting and underfitting risks. We compare several LSTM
variants and hybrid architectures, provide implementation de-
tails, hyperparameter choices, and reproducible artifacts.
Index Terms—time series forecasting, LSTM, attention, multi-
variate forecasting, commodity prices, India
I. INTRODUCTION
A. Time Series Prediction
Time series prediction involves the analysis of sequential
data to forecast future values. It is widely applied in predicting
stock prices, commodity prices, and other temporal phenom-
ena.
B. Background and Problem Statement
During the COVID-19 pandemic, many MSMEs struggled
due to:
•Lack of crisis planning skills.
•Fluctuating commodity prices leading to temporary busi-
ness closures.
•Declining turnover.
1) COVID-19 Impact on MSMEs:The pandemic disrupted
supply chains and demand, causing drastic commodity price
fluctuations.
2) Russia–Ukraine Conflict and Oil Price Volatility:Since
2022 the Russia–Ukraine conflict increased volatility in oil and
grain markets, raising transport and production costs.
3) Climate Change and Extreme Weather:Extreme weather
events have increased supply shocks for agricultural commodi-
ties.
4) India’s Agricultural Price Instability:Staple commodi-
ties such as onions, tomatoes, and pulses often experience
sudden swings due to seasonality, supply inefficiencies, and
policy interventions.
C. Proposed Solution
We develop a multivariate LSTM-based forecasting frame-
work (and variants) to provide early warning and stabilize
MSME decision-making.

II. THEORETICALBACKGROUND
A. Deep Learning and LSTM
Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) [1] mitigates vanishing
gradients and captures long-term dependencies, making it
suitable for multivariate time series.
B. Attention and CNN–LSTM Hybrids
Attention mechanisms help models focus on important
timesteps [2], while CNN-LSTM hybrids extract local patterns
before temporal modeling.
C. Batch Normalization & Optimization
Batch Normalization stabilizes training [3]. We compare
SGD and Adam [4] optimizers; Adam performed best in our
experiments.

III. METHODOLOGY
A. Data Collection and Description
We used an Indian commodity price dataset (daily fre-
quency) containing price series for multiple commodities
(e.g., rice, wheat, onions, tomatoes, pulses, edible oil). Each
row includesdateand commodity price columns (11 target
commodities in the experiments).
Dataset source and license:“Indian commodity prices
dataset, Government of India Open Data Portal (data.gov.in),
2024 release, OGD License.”
B. Preprocessing
•Convertdateto DateTime and sort chronologically.
•Fill / interpolate missing values (forward-fill for short
gaps; consider seasonal interpolation for longer gaps).
•Min–Max scaling to [0,1].
•Create sliding windows of lengthT= 30for multi-output
next-step forecasting.
•Train/test split: 80% train, 20% test (chronological split).

VII. DISCUSSION
The multivariate LSTM with Adam and BatchNorm
achieved the best short-term (1–10 day) prediction accuracy.
Attention and Conv-LSTM variants further enhanced learn-
ing of cross-commodity dependencies. Batch normalization
consistently improved stability and convergence. For long-
horizon forecasting (e.g., 30 days), error accumulation remains
a limitation; future work may use seq2seq or Transformer-
based architectures.
VIII. CONCLUSION
This study successfully developed and evaluated multiple
LSTM-based architectures for forecasting commodity prices
across 11 essential commodities in India. Using 1,460 daily
records, we demonstrated that the Attention LSTM model
achieved superior performance with a validation loss of
0.018162, MAE of 0.027, RMSE of 0.039, andR 2 of 0.91,
outperforming standard LSTM, Bi-LSTM, and Conv-LSTM
variants. The model was trained for up to 200 epochs using
the Adam optimizer with adaptive learning rate schedul-
ing, effectively capturing complex temporal dependencies and
inter-commodity correlations. Key contributions include: (1) a
comprehensive comparison of LSTM variants on multivari-
ate commodity price data, (2) robust training methodology
with Batch Normalization and gradient clipping to prevent
overfitting, and (3) empirical validation demonstrating that
attention mechanisms significantly improve prediction accu-
racy for multidimensional time series. The framework provides
MSMEs with actionable early-warning capabilities to antici-
pate price fluctuations during volatile market conditions such
as pandemics, geopolitical conflicts, and climate events. Future
work will explore Transformer-based architectures for longer
forecasting horizons and incorporate exogenous variables such
as weather patterns, policy changes, and global market indi-
cators to further enhance predictive accuracy.
