1. Introduction.
Hello everyone. Today I am presenting our work on Magneto-Inductive Communications for.
Littoral Environments..
Traditional underwater communication, like acoustics and radio waves, performs poorly in.
shallow water because of multipath, noise, and high attenuation.
Magneto-Inductive, or Ml communication, provides an alternative by using stable, low-frequency
magnetic fields that can pass through water, sand, and sediment with much less disturbance
2. MI Propagation Principles
MI communication works on the idea of a magnetic dipole created by a coil..
Because these magnetic fields are quasi-static, they don't create multipath reflections like.
acoustic waves.
This gives MI systems predictable behavior, low attenuation, and even covert operation because
the electric radiation is minimal..
3. System Architecture
The MI transmitter includes a high-Q coil, a resonant driver, and sometimes a magnetic core to
boost performance.e
The receiver uses a multi-turn coil, a resonance circuit, and a low-noise amplifier to detect very
weak magnetic signals.
Together, these components allow reliable communication in harsh littoral conditions.
4. Modulation
MI systems usually use ASK, FSK, PSK, or MSK modulation.
At frequencies below 6 kHz, we typically achieve 10 to 500 bps, which is suitable for underwater
sensing, short messages, and navigation.
5. Historical Experiments
Several important field tests established the reliability of Ml communication.
In 1997, signals were successfully transmitted over more than 1300 yards in shallow water.
In 1998, seabed tests showed ranges beyond 300 meters, and in 2001 UBV-sized systems
achieved 16-300 bps over 250-400 meters.
6. Major Advances (2005-2025)
Recent research introduced compact coil designs, metamaterial resonators, relay-aided
networks, and MI waveguides made of passive resonant loops.
MI is also used for underwater localization, achieving 0.5 to 1 meter accuracy, even where
acoustic systems fail.
7. Challenges
Despite its advantages, MI has limitations such as 1/R3 signal decay, narrow bandwidth, and
sensitivity to coil orientation.
Environmental conductivity also affects performance.
8. Conclusion
In summary, MI communication offers a stable, predictable, and energy-efficient solution for
shallow-water and mixed-media environments.
With recent improvements, it is becoming a key technology for underwater networks,
autonomous systems, and future littoral communication applications.

Problem: Low Earth Orbit (LEO) satellite populations are exploding from approximately 9,000 in
2025 to over 50,000 by 2030. Traditional pairwise collision analysis has O(n?) complexity,
making it computationally impossible at this scale. Small debris remains difficult to detect, and
deterministic distance thresholds ignore inherent TLE (Two-Line Element) uncertainty.
Solution: A complete software-only system combining SGP4 orbit propagation, Graph Neural
uncertainty quantification using 1,000+ samples per satellite pair, and a production-ready 3D
interactive web dashboard with real-time alerts.
Key Innovation: GNN replaces traditional O(n?) pairwise analysis with O(n) sparse graph
approach, enabling scalability to 50,o00+ objects while maintaining high accuracy
Impact: Directly supports LEO safety for megaconstellations, aligns with India's Space
Situational Awareness (SSA) needs, and is highly publishable with proven metrics (mAP50
0.75, 7-day advance prediction).
Feasibility: Fully software-only with zero hardware requirements, uses free open data from
CelesTrak, leverages mature libraries (SGP4, PyTorch Geometric), and has a concrete 4-week
implementation roadmap.

Abstract
The study addresses the issue faced by MSMEs in Indonesia during the pandemic, where
erratic commodity prices severely affected their operations. A deep learning model based on
Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) is proposed to predict commodity prices and help MSMEs
make informed decisions. The model used multivariate time series data collected from PIHPS
Nasional and includes features like rice, chicken meat, eggs, and several types of chilies.
Key Achievements:.
Best MAE: 255.998
Orbit Propagation Module
Algorithm: SGP4 (Simple General Perturbation 4) and SDP4 for deep space
Libraries: Python sgp4, astropy, skyfield
Output: Earth-Centered Inertial (ECl) coordinates (x, y, z in km) and velocity (vx, vy, vz.
in km/s)
Performance: Can propagate 10,000 satellites in under 1 second
Conjunction Engine Module.
Two-Stage Filtering:
1. Coarse filter: Distance threshold 10 km (eliminates 99% of pairs, O(n).
complexity)
2. Precise filter: Relative velocity >5 km/s, altitude difference <50 km.
Min Separation Calculation: Numerical integration using Runge-Kutta 4 method with
gradient-based optimization.
Collision Probability: Monte Carlo with 1,000 samples, 3o covariance ellipses.
Risk LeveIs: LOW (<0.1), MEDIUM (0.1-0.3), HIGH (0.3-0.7), CRITICAL (>0.7)
Uncertainty Quantification Module.
. Monte Carlo Pipeline:
1. Sample TLE uncertainty: Bstar drag term 5%, mean motion +0.0001 rev/day,
inclination 0.001
2. Propagate 1,000 samples using SGP4 for each satellite
3. Compute covariance matrix (33) from position samples
4. Extract 3o ellipse radii from eigenvalues
5. Collision if distance between means < combined 3o radius
Output: Mean position, covariance matrix, ellipse radii, collision probability.
Layer 3: ML Layer (Detailed).
Graph Neural Network (GNN) Risk Model
Core Innovation: Solves O(n?) bottleneck by modeling satellites as graph nodes and.
proximity pairs as edges
Node Features (9 per satellite): x, y, z, vx, vy, vz, mass, area, bstar
Edge Features (3 per pair): distance, relative_velocity, angle
Graph Construction: Sparse graph with edges only for pairs within 10 km (reduces
edges from n? to ~100n)
Architecture:
GraphConv(9 > 64) > ReLU > Dropout(0.2)
GraphConv(64 > 128) -> ReLU > Dropout(0.2)
GraphConv(128 -> 64) -> ReLU
Model configuration: Attention multivariate LSTM with Adam optimizer, Batchnorm layer,
reduced LSTM layer, optimized hidden size, and grouped features.

1. Introduction
With the rapid advancement of Industry 4.0, smart cities, and cyber-physical systems, Digital
Twins have become a foundational technology for representing, monitoring, and optimizing
real-world systems in virtual environments. A digital twin establishes a continuous, bidirectional
connection between a physical system and its virtual counterpart, enabling real-time monitoring,
simulation, and decision support.
However, most existing digital twin platforms rely on 2D dashboards or static 3D CAD.
models, which are insufficient for understanding complex spatial structures, dense.
environments, and dynamic system behavior. These limitations reduce situational awareness
At the same time, Knowledge Graphs (KGs) provide a structured semantic representation of
entities, attributes, and relationships, enabling intelligent querying, reasoning, and inference.
diagrams, disconnected from real-world geometry..
Meanwhile, modern sensing technologies such as LiDAR and photogrammetry generate
dense 3D point cloud data, offering highly accurate geometric representations of real
environments. However, interacting with massive point clouds using traditional interfaces is
computationally expensive and cognitively challenging.
This project proposes an Immersive Digital Twin Platform that integrates Knowledge Graph
reasoning with real-time 3D point cloud visualization inside VR/AR environments, enabling
intuitive, semantic, and spatial interaction with complex digital twins..
2. Core Problem Definition (What Exactly Is the Problem?).
Despite advances in digital twin technology, three fundamental problems remain unresolved:
1. Geometry-Semantics Disconnection
Geometric data (3D models, point clouds) and semantic data (knowledge graphs, rules)
2.
Non-Immersive Interaction
Traditional 2D dashboards and flat 3D views fail to convey spatial complexity, reducing
human understanding and increasing cognitive load.
3. Scalability & Performance Challenges
Real-time rendering and interaction with large-scale point cloud data is computationally
expensive and poorly supported in immersive systems.
These issues significantly limit the practical use of digital twins in smart manufacturing,
infrastructure monitoring, smart cities, and cyber-physical systems.
3. Problem Statement
Traditional digital twin systems suffer from:
Limited spatial understanding due to non-immersive visualization
Weak integration between geometric data and semantic knowledge
Difficulty handling large-scale 3D point cloud data in real time.
Lack of intuitive interfaces for querying and reasoning over system knowledge.
As a result, digital twins often function as passive visual replicas rather than intelligent,
interactive systems..
4. Proposed Solution.
This project introduces an immersive, intelligence-driven digital twin platform that:
Represents real-world geometry using real-time 3D point clouds.
Encodes system knowledge using a Knowledge Graph
Synchronizes geometry, semantics, and live sensor data.
Provides VR/AR-based immersive interaction for exploration, analysis, and.
decision-making
The proposed solution bridges the gap between physical realism, semantic intelligence, and
human-centered interaction.

Design and Fabrication of a Flexible
Respiration (Breathing) Sensor with
Embedded Data Acquisition and Machine
Learning-Based Analytics
Abstract
This project presents the design and implementation of a flexible respiration monitoring system
that measures human breathing patterns using a wearable sensor. The system integrates.
embedded data acquisition and machine learning-based analytics to estimate respiratory rate
and classify breathing behavior. It provides a low-cost, comfortable, and real-time solution for
respiration monitoring..
Description
Respiration monitoring is an important physiological parameter used in healthcare, fitness, and.
uncomfortable for continuous use.
In this project, a flexible respiration sensor is designed and embedded into a wearable strap
to sense chest expansion and contraction during breathing. The sensor output is processed
using an embedded microcontroller that performs data acquisition and preprocessing. Machine
Iearning algorithms are then applied to analyze the breathing signal and extract meaningful
information such as respiratory rate and breathing patterns.
The system emphasizes comfort, portability, low power consumption, and intelligent data
analysis.
Implementation Details.
1. Sensor Design and Fabrication.
A flexible piezoresistive/stretch sensor is mounted on an elastic chest strap..
Chest movement during breathing causes the sensor to stretch.
2. Signal Conditioning.
Analog filters are applied to remove noise and motion artifacts..
The conditioned signal represents the breathing waveform.
3. Embedded Data Acquisition
The conditioned analog signal is sampled using the ADC of a microcontroller..
Sampling rate is maintained at 25-50 Hz, sufficient for respiration signals.
Digital preprocessing such as filtering and normalization is performed.
4. Machine Learning Analytics.
Preprocessed data is segmented into fixed time windows.
A lightweight machine learning model (1D-CNN or LSTM) is trained..
The model estimates respiratory rate and classifies breathing patterns (normal, fast,
shallow, apnea)..
5. Output and Communication.
The results are displayed as breaths per minute (BPM).
Data can be transmitted wirelessly to a mobile or PC application for visualization and
storage.

Vision Statement:
To transform passive video-based learning into active, immersive
knowledge exploration by combining Al-powered concept extraction with.
interactive 3D concept maps in Virtual Reality.
Why This Matters
Traditional educational videos:.
o Force learners into linear consumption.
o Hide conceptual dependencies
o Encourage passive viewing
o Make revision time-consuming and inefficient.
This project addresses these limitations by enabling non-linear,
exploratory learning inside VR..
Problem statement:
'Immersive VR Concept Map Across Different Learning Contexts'
Paper Analysis (Key Points)
Problem Addressed: Current video-based learning suffers from:.
1. Linear constraints - Students cannot easily navigate to specific concepts
2. Hidden relationships - Prerequisite dependencies are implicit.
3. Low engagement - Passive consumption reduces retention.
4. Inefficient review - Time-consuming to find and revisit content
Solution: Augment videos with immersive VR concept maps dynamically generated
from knowledge graphs..
An intelligent three-stage system that:.
1. Extracts concepts from educational videos using AI (NLP)
2.Builds semantic knowledge graphs with prerequisite relationships
3. Renders interactive 3D concept maps in VR for immersive exploration
Key Innovations:
1. Automated Knowledge Extraction - Al processes videos without manual effort.
2. Standards-Based - W3C Web Annotation + RDF compliance
3. Progressive Construction - Graph builds in real-time during video playback
4. Non-Linear Navigation - Students control their learning path
5. Research-Validated - 61-participant study with published results
o System Architecture.

Hypotheses:
H1: No significant difference in comprehension performance between.
groups
H2: User experience dimensions may vary based on contextual factors
Implementation:
o Extract concepts + prerequisite relations from video transcripts..
o Encode them in JSON-LD / RDF using SKOS + Web Annotation Ontology..
o Render them in Unreal Engine 5.3 as interactive 3D spheres linked in space..
o Learners can revisit video segments by selecting nodes..
User Study: Two groups (postgraduates vs. undergraduates) tested with different topics
(photogrammetry vs. cloud computing).
o Result: Comprehension similar across groups, but user experience varied.
(fatigue, timing, background)..
Contribution: Proof-of-concept that immersive VR concept maps enhance engagement
and can be adapted across contexts..
