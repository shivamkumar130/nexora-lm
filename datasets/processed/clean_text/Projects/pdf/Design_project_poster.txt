Abstract
This project presents a lightweight, reproducible pipeline
for detecting shoplifting behaviour in surveillance video
using the classification variant of YOLOv8. The workflow
includes frame extraction, dataset creation, model fine-
tuning, per-frame inference, and temporal aggregation to
generate robust video-level decisions.
Objectives &
Contributions
•Reproducible end-to-end pipeline us-
ing YOLOv8 (classification).
•Frame-level classification & temporal
aggregation for video-level decisions.
•Empirical demonstration of improve-
ment after fine-tuning (baseline→
fine-tuned).
•Public scripts for data prep, training,
testing and evaluation.

Inference & Postprocessing
Inference Steps:
•Preprocess: resize to 224×224, apply Im-
ageNet normalization.
•Forward pass through YOLOv8 model; ap-
ply confidence thresholding.
•Annotate frames — Normal and Shoplift-
ing.
•Save annotated output video tooutput-
s/custom_result.mp4.
•Aggregate results per video (majority vote
or confidence-weighted) to compute preci-
sion, recall, and F1.

Discussion, Limitations & Future Work
Key points
•Frame-only classification misses short-duration gestures — add temporal models.
•Fine-tuning corrected baseline class bias and produced balanced predictions.
•Lightweight (6.2M params) suitable for edge deployment; temporal enrichment recommended.
Future enhancements
•Temporal models (LSTM / Transformer) for sequence reasoning.
•Detection + tracking (YOLOv8 + DeepSORT) to localize hands/objects.
•Dataset expansion (>1000 videos) and ONNX/TensorRT edge optimization.
Conclusion & Acknowledgment
Conclusion
Fine-tuning YOLOv8 classification on curated surveillance frames improved frame-level accuracy from 48.65% to 85.95% (+37.30 pp). The pipeline is a lightweight,
real-time-capable baseline; next steps include temporal modeling and dataset expansion.
